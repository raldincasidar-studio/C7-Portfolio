The goal is to create a fully functional, responsive, and branded e-commerce platform that effectively showcases C7 Convenience Store's offerings, locations, and community commitment in Cagayan de Oro.

1. Project Overview

Develop a modern, user-friendly website for C7 Convenience Store, incorporating online shopping capabilities, a detailed store locator, and a strong brand presence. The website must be responsive across all devices, with a focus on mobile-first optimization after initial desktop implementation.

3. Branding Guidelines

Adherence to C7 Convenience Store's visual identity is paramount. The AI agent must strictly follow these guidelines:

3.1. Color Palette

•
Primary Brand Color: A vibrant, fresh green (e.g., #66CC00). This color should be used for primary calls-to-action, key headings, and elements requiring emphasis.

•
Secondary Accent Color: Clean white (#FFFFFF) for backgrounds, text on dark elements, and general contrast.

•
Neutral Tones: Utilize shades of grey for text, borders, and subtle background elements to maintain a clean and modern aesthetic.

•
Dark Grey for primary text: #333333

•
Medium Grey for secondary text/icons: #666666

•
Light Grey for backgrounds/borders: #EEEEEE



3.2. Typography

•
Primary Font: A modern, clean, and highly readable sans-serif typeface (e.g., 'Roboto', 'Open Sans', 'Lato'). Use for body text and general content.

•
Secondary Font: A slightly bolder or distinct sans-serif for headlines and call-to-action elements (e.g., 'Montserrat', 'Oswald'). This creates visual hierarchy and brand recognition.

3.3. Logo Style

•
The C7 logo features a distinct diamond shape, primarily in the vibrant green, with the letter 'C' and the number '7' integrated within it. The overall style is modern, simple, and easily recognizable.

•
The logo must be prominently displayed in the header (left-aligned) and footer. Ensure proper scaling and clear visibility across all device sizes.

3.4. Imagery Style

•
General: Bright, clean, well-lit photos of store interiors, product displays, and friendly staff. Emphasize a welcoming and organized environment.

•
Lifestyle: Images featuring people enjoying products or interacting with the store in a positive, convenient manner, reflecting the local Cagayan de Oro community.

•
Product Photography: Clear, high-resolution images of products, especially for the online shopping section. Consistency in lighting and background is crucial.

4. Technical Requirements

4.1. Responsive Design

•
The website must be fully responsive, adapting seamlessly to various screen sizes (desktops, tablets, mobile phones).

•
Development Approach: Implement the desktop version first, then rigorously optimize and refine for mobile devices, ensuring a mobile-first user experience.

4.2. Online Shopping (E-commerce Integration)

•
Implement a robust e-commerce system allowing customers to browse products, add items to a cart, and complete secure purchases.

•
Features: Product listings with images, descriptions, pricing, quantity selection, shopping cart functionality, secure checkout process, order confirmation.

•
Product Data: The system should be capable of handling product categories, search functionality, and inventory management (even if initially placeholder data).

4.3. Store Locations & Interactive Map

•
Integrate an interactive map (e.g., Google Maps API) on the
Locations page, allowing users to easily find branches. Each branch listing should link to its location on the map.

4.4. Performance & SEO

•
Optimize for fast loading times and smooth user experience.

•
Implement basic SEO best practices (semantic HTML, meta tags, friendly URLs).

4.5. Security

•
Ensure secure data handling for online transactions and user information.

•
Implement HTTPS.

5. UI/UX Design Guidelines

5.1. Layout & Spacing

•
Clean and Uncluttered: Utilize ample white space to improve readability and focus on content.

•
Grid-based Layouts: Employ consistent grid systems for structured and organized content presentation.

•
Visual Hierarchy: Clearly differentiate primary, secondary, and tertiary information through size, color, and placement.

5.2. Color Usage

•
Strictly adhere to the defined color palette. The vibrant green should be used strategically to highlight key elements and calls-to-action, not to overwhelm the design.

•
Ensure sufficient color contrast for accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA standards).

5.3. Shadow Rules

•
Use subtle shadows for depth and emphasis on interactive elements (buttons, cards, modals) to indicate interactivity and hierarchy.

•
Avoid heavy or distracting shadows. A consistent, light shadow style should be applied throughout the site.

5.4. Focus on User Experience (UX)

•
Intuitive Navigation: Clear and consistent navigation across all pages. Users should easily understand where they are and how to get to other sections.

•
Ease of Use: Simple and straightforward interactions, especially for the online shopping and checkout process.

•
Feedback: Provide clear visual feedback for user actions (e.g., button hovers, form submissions).

•
Accessibility: Design with accessibility in mind, ensuring the site is usable by a wide range of users.

5.5. Funnel Focus for Sales & Branding

•
Homepage: Designed to quickly engage users, highlight key offerings (24/7, locations, products), and guide them towards product browsing or location finding.

•
Product Pages: Clear product information, high-quality images, easy "Add to Cart" functionality, and related product suggestions to encourage purchases.

•
Checkout Process: Streamlined and intuitive, minimizing steps and distractions to reduce cart abandonment.

•
Branding Reinforcement: Consistent use of logo, colors, and tone across all pages to reinforce brand identity and trust.

6. Development Workflow

1.
Desktop Version First: Develop the full website functionality and design for desktop screens.

2.
Mobile-First Optimization: After desktop implementation, thoroughly review and optimize the entire website for mobile devices, ensuring an excellent user experience on smaller screens.

3.
Testing: Implement rigorous testing for functionality, responsiveness, cross-browser compatibility, and performance.

Fully functional and responsive website code.

•
Integration of online shopping capabilities.

•
Implementation of the interactive store locator.

•
Adherence to all specified branding and UI/UX guidelines.

•
A brief report on the development process and any deviations or challenges encountered.
